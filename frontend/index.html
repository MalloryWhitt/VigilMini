Visualize lobbying influence through relationships.

Vigil Mini is a civic-tech prototype that uses the official Senate Lobbying Disclosure Act (LDA) filings API to visualize connections between lobbying firms (registrants), their clients, and the lobbyists listed on filings. The goal is to help researchers, journalists, and the public explore influence networks with a simple interface.

Open the Explorer API Docs

What the graph represents

Each node and edge in the graph corresponds directly to information disclosed in U.S. Senate Lobbying Disclosure Act filings.

Lobbying firms (registrants), their clients, and listed lobbyists are connected exactly as reported across filings. The visualization highlights structure, repetition, and central actors without inferring intent or influence.

How to explore the data

  • Entity search — companies, lobbying firms, or individual lobbyists
  • Topic search — policy issues such as “defense”, “privacy”, or “semiconductor”
  • Filing year filter — narrow scope or compare activity over time

Scope and planned expansion

Vigil Mini is an early-stage public-interest prototype focused on retrieval and discovery. It reflects what is explicitly disclosed in official filings and does not perform automated inference.

The broader Vigil project will evolve into a smarter investigative platform, incorporating NLP with spaCy, integration with Congress.gov, and cross-dataset analysis to support deeper research workflows.

Data source: U.S. Senate LDA filings API . This tool is for exploration and research support.
